## 1. spelt-white.png — כוסמין לבן רך (60% white / 40% white spelt)

Photograph a round artisan sourdough boule cut cleanly in half. One half rests
flat on the board with the open crumb facing the camera, the other half stands
upright just behind it with the scored crust facing forward — both crust and
crumb clearly visible in the same frame.

Photograph a round artisan soft white-spelt sourdough boule cut cleanly in half,
with one half flat (crumb up) and one half upright behind (crust forward) —
both visible in the same frame.

Use the attached image precisely as the style anchor: match the exact surface,
lighting, camera angle, framing, and color grade.

This specific loaf is a soft white-spelt sourdough (mostly white flour with a
portion of sifted white spelt): the crust is a pale golden-tan with a gentle
rounded shape and a single soft, modest score, the crumb is fine and evenly
aerated with small-to-medium holes, and the interior is a warm cream-white —
delicate, soft, and forgiving.

Surface: light natural-grain oak cutting board on a creamy off-white linen cloth.
Background fades to soft beige bokeh.

Composition: three-quarter overhead angle, about 30° downward tilt, both halves
occupying the center 65% of the frame with generous breathing room.
Shot on an 85mm lens at f/4 — shallow but not extreme depth of field.

Lighting: soft directional natural light from the upper-left, late-morning
quality, gentle warm shadow falling to the lower-right.

Style: clean editorial bakery photography, warm earth tones, no flour scatter,
no props, no hands. Color grade: warm cream highlights, soft chocolate shadows,
slightly desaturated for a refined hand-baked feel.
